Get Better At Public Speaking

Practice Time: The More Time The Better!
Confidence Rating: 100% Confidence In You!

Do you struggle speaking in front of an audience or talking to large groups of people? You're not alone! Studies show that anywhere from 72%-75% of people struggle with this just like you. To make public speaking a bit easier, I've made an easy step by step guide so that you can get better at public speaking!

  • Step By Step

  • Plan Out Your Speech
  • Learn Who Your Audience Is
  • Use Positive Body Language
  • Be Comfortable With Silence
  • Enjoy Yourself!

  1. Plan Out Your Speech

    Spend time putting your speech together in advance so that it flows naturally when you read it in front of your audience. The more you plan, the less stressed out you'll be the day of the speech. Whether you memorize your speech or read it from a paper, the more you practice, the more likely it is that your brain will be able to catch itself if you happen to make a mistake when reading.

  2. Learn Who Your Audience Is

    Think about why you are giving the speech in the first place.The more you can relate to your audience and/or their interests, the easier giving your speech will be! It may even be like talking to a friend.

  3. Use Positive Body Language

    Look your audience in the eye, and use both facial as well as hand expressions. If you're reading your speech, make eye contact for a few words or a sentence before looking back at your paper; When you look back up, choose someone else in another direction to make eye contact with. If you've memorized our speech, still do this step, but of course skip out on looking down at your paper (since you don't have one!). Doing this shows your audience that you are speaking to them, not just reading.

    Speak about something you're passionate about! The more passion you have about a topic, the better the audience will recieve your speech and the more fun you'll have delivering it. It's okay to be excited! The tone of your voice can go a long way in controlling how your speech makes the audience feel!

  4. Be Comfortable With Silence

    Silence isnt a bad thing! Silence allows you to stop, take a second, and continue. By panicking when theres a pause in your speech, you could come off as nervous and rushed (even if you are nervous!). If you are speaking and want something you just said to really sink in, silence is the best way to allow that.

  5. Enjoy Yourself!

    Be proud of yourself. You have an audience of people want to hear what you have to say! As mentioned in previous steps, the more fun you're having, the easier giving the speech will be. The more passion behind the speech, the more both you and your audience will enjoy it.
